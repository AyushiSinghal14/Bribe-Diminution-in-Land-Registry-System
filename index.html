Bribe Diminution in Land Registry System

The land registry system is designed to ensure that all land transactions are conducted fairly and transparently. However, there have been instances of bribery and corruption in the system, which have led to the diminution of its effectiveness. In order to combat this problem, this system is being processed.

Involvement of middlemen & brokers

The involvement of middlemen and brokers in the land registry system can lead to corruption and inefficiency. Middlemen and brokers may charge high fees for their services, and they may use their position to influence the outcome of land registry procedures.

In addition, middlemen and brokers may be able to imposters posing as the seller of property. Land registry takes a considerably long time to complete title registration. Many legal problems can also arise during this long gap.

Increasing number of fraud cases and time delays

The increasing number of fraud cases is causing delays in the processing of claims. In some cases, it can take months or even years to resolve a fraud case.

This is due to the fact that there are a limited number of investigators and prosecutors who can work on these cases. Additionally, the process of gathering evidence and interviewing witnesses can be time-consuming.
